I am an Associate Professor of Linguistics and Data Science at New York University, and a Research Scientist at Google. At NYU, I direct the Computation and Psycholinguistics Lab; we use behavioral experiments and computational methods to study how people learn and understand language. At both Google and NYU, I work on large language model evaluation and interpretability.

I am accepting PhD students for Fall 2026 through either Linguistics or Data Science; I look forward to reading your applications! Please read the FAQ before contacting me about PhD admissions.

I am currently hiring one or more post-docs to work on projects related to cognitively plausible LLMs, through interpretability, training data, and modeling. Apply here.
